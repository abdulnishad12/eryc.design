FEW THINGS I’VE LEARNED DURING THE YEAR OF 2018

  • Don't plan it — Do it.

    We plan for success, trying to get all the variables in the right places—if this happens, we do that. Thing is: we can’t be sure of the future, nor the problems life will throw our way. So spend as little time as possible planning and get to work. You’ll find the solutions for the problems way faster.

  • ROUTINES AREN’T THAT BAD—IN FACT, THEY’RE QUITE USEFUL.

    Routines are very useful—especially if you’re working remotely. It helps you free the cognitive load of decision-making for the first few hours of your day. This means you can actually make time for things that matter to you, like reading, going to the gym, prepping a meal, spending time with your family or watching that documentary.

  • GRATITUDE IS FUEL.

    Being grateful creates a positive shift in your mind. Instead of looking at things as a burden, you get to spot opportunities. Which will “trick” your mind to believe you’re performing a happy job, when you might be going through a painful moment.

  • NEVER STOP LEARNING.

    Going to college isn’t enough. This is true especially in the tech industry, where everything changes from night to day. But more important than just knowing what’s new, is actually learning how to create the new. Thankfully there are many resources nowdays: youtube, blogs, podcasts, books, courses.

  • SET HUGE GOALS TO ACHIEVE BIG RESULTS.

    This is a hack. And it works like this: whatever goal you have, make it 10x bigger. You will still try to achieve it, and you will likely fail. But even if you only achieve half of the 10x goal, it’ll be better than achieving half of 1x. And believe me, it works.

  • FOCUS IS LIKE A MUSCLE, AND YOU HAVE TO TRAIN IT EVERY DAY.

    Having focus is essential in order to deliver the jobs we — as creatives — took the responsibility. And the liberty of remote work plus the subjectiveness of creative work creates all sorts of problems, such as: procrastination and wrongly set deadlines. It is our responsibility to focus on the job to be done. And although this is hard — if you’re not used to —, it is something that can be trained.

  • LACK OF SLEEP IS THE ENEMY OF: PRODUCTIVITY AND CREATIVITY.

    Sleep is important. It’s the time of the day when the body starts recovering from the day’s activities and recharging for tomorrow. Not getting enough sleep will affect your mood, your decision-making, your memory and your diet, all of the things which are crucial for you to have a productive and creative day.

  • EXERCISING GENERATES ENERGY.

    People often think that going hard and spending so much energy on the gym will make you tired. It might on your first week or two, but once you get into the routine, you’ll notice a boost in energy. That’s because exercising releases endorphins, helps you sleep better, helps manage stress and trains the heart to work more efficiently and pump more oxygen to the brain and other organs.

  • GIVE IN TO YOUR EMOTIONS.

    We humans are fueled by emotions. Showing your emotions helps create empathy, which leads to long-lasting relationships. This is helpful even when it comes to businesses, since every business is run by another human.
